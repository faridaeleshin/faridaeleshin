About

I design technologies, frameworks, and policies that promote equity, responsibility, and accountability in computing. My work takes a human-centered approach to understand how people—particularly underserved communities—interact with digital systems. I explore the security and privacy challenges, values, and practices that shape technology adoption and its impact on society.

Drawing on insights from Human-Computer Interaction, Responsible AI, Social Computing, AI Governance, Ethics, and Security and Privacy research, I create socio-technical interventions that address inequities and foster responsible technology use. These include tools for equitable access, responsible AI audits, digital literacy platforms, and community-driven systems for safety and trust.

Ultimately, my goal is to amplify user voices in the design and governance of emerging technologies and to inform institutional practices that make computing more just, inclusive, and socially accountable. I am advised by Cori Faklaris and part of the Security and Privacy Experiences (SPEX) Lab.
